# Origin

Seeded from a conversation between Artem Zhiganov and Viktor Shiryaev on 20 April 2026, after roughly four years out of regular contact. Raw Russian transcript in `transcripts/viktor-2026-04-20.md` (gitignored).

## What was actually talked about

### Metacrisis — as hypothesis, not given

Neither participant treats metacrisis as settled doctrine. Viktor is currently skeptical of the strongest versions of the claim — cites Alexey Kapterev's empirical challenge ("name one parameter that is actually worse than before"), worries about apophenia, is wary of paid courses that treat the frame as proven. Artem is more persuaded (via Schmachtenberger, Scott Alexander's *Moloch*, time at Newspeak House) but agrees the frame is hard to measure and easy to over-read.

Both agree, independently of whether the frame is correct: things can be better, and the interesting question is how to act with real agency at more than individual scale.

### Artem's framing — three circles

A Venn diagram as a working frame:

1. **Personal awareness** — mindfulness, inner work
2. **Collective coordination** — protocols, shared practices
3. **Impact / resources** — where attention and material flows actually go

Named intersections:

- *awareness ∩ impact* → **rigid-purpose**
- *impact ∩ coordination* → **protocols**

When all three are active you get something worth calling agency, stewardship, or — the word Artem argued for specifically — **solidarity**. Not the leftist/hippie connotation, but the sense that built Solidarność (mostly not left) and that materialises as mutual-aid funds (касса взаимопомощи). Artem notes the absence of any such fund for Russian-speaking immigrants four years into the war, despite mutualism being a 19th-century tradition (Proudhon, Kropotkin).

### Viktor's direction — "Sōka Gakkai in a new key"

Viktor articulated what he now actively wants and does not want:

- **Not:** online schools; horizontal "let's meditate together" circles; teacher-led formats where he is the draw. Explicitly: these drain him, generate little impact, and haven't produced emergent community.
- **Yes:** a quasi-order — shared principles, shared practices, a commons — that others can hold independently of him. Reference forms: Order of Interbeing (Plum Village / Thich Nhat Hanh), Sōka Gakkai (with reservations about sectarianism), AA as pure organisational-design exemplar.
- **Tone:** "guerrilla activism" — seed envelopes buried in gardens, meditation cards dropped into mailboxes, an open kit anyone can download.
- **Philosophical spine:** Buddhist — three truths, emptiness, interbeing, skilful means, non-self, bodhisattva ideal — translated into contemporary language, not preserved in religious form.
- **Funding form:** Patreon / donations / grants / NGO, not the "close €X of participant revenue by month-end" grind.

Viktor framed this as translating Chan/Mahayana grounds into present-day collective-action language.

### Principles, not values

Both emphasised this distinction hard. Values ("it is nice when X") are fragile — pressure-test them and they dissolve into emotion. Principles are load-bearing. Contemporary exemplar: AA's 12 Steps (referenced as "заповеди" — commandments — in the conversation). Historical case: the Russian Old Believers — post-raskol forced decentralisation produced mutual-aid funds, a serious work ethic, and by the early 19th century the pooled capital was being used to buy peasants out of serfdom.

### Belief systems ↔ governance

Both see religions — and more broadly belief systems — as governance systems. Artem: the loss of principled governance at the state level is one of the clearest symptoms you could point at for the metacrisis claim; contemporary Russian state ideology is a tragic illustration (principle-free, grievance-driven, no positive horizon).

### Offline / embodied community

Viktor: offline community is the missing piece and its value only grows as digitisation and atomisation deepen. References include Newspeak House, Rufus Pollock's place in France, the Lindisfarne Association (William Irwin Thompson's 1970s Canadian crucible), Plum Village. Ten days without phones. Artem pointed to Climate Fresk as the right reference for workshop *form* — an open-source facilitation kit anyone can download, run by certified facilitators for free worldwide, concrete and collaborative rather than lecture-shaped.

### A line worth keeping

Artem (via *Pandemic*, the cooperative board game): *no one can win alone; only everyone can lose.* Viktor said he'd be carrying that one.
