# research

Notes and source material feeding the concept track. Organised by theme; each topic gets its own file when there's enough to draft.

## Commons and mutualism

- **Elinor Ostrom** — *Governing the Commons* (1990); Nobel 2009 for demonstrating commons as a viable third way beyond market and state.
- **Pierre-Joseph Proudhon** — *What is Property?* (1840); mutualism as a 19th-century precursor of commons thinking.
- **Peter Kropotkin** — *Mutual Aid: A Factor of Evolution* (1902).
- **Max Stirner**, **the 19th-century anarchist cluster** — as "advanced governance thinkers" (Artem): the people who thought hardest about coordination without coercion.
- **Kassa vzaimopomoshchi / mutual aid fund** — concrete 19th-century mechanism; open question why none exists for Russian-speaking emigration.

## Metacrisis — as contested frame

The repo treats metacrisis as a hypothesis to probe, not as established fact.

- **Daniel Schmachtenberger** — Lex Fridman interview (2020) as entry point; popularised "massive coordination failure."
- **Scott Alexander** — *Meditations on Moloch* (2014); multi-agent rationality producing civilisational loss.
- **Empirical critics** — Alexey Kapterev's challenge: which specific parameters are objectively worse than before? Apophenia as a risk of the frame.
- The question of whether the frame can be measured prospectively vs only retrospectively.

## Principle-based organisations

- **Alcoholics Anonymous** — the 12 Steps; global reach from one book; durably decentralised; appeal to a "higher power" as structural (not dogmatic). Flagged as the strongest contemporary exemplar.
- **Russian Old Believers** — post-raskol forced decentralisation → mutual-aid funds → work ethic → industrial capitalisation → financing of serf emancipation.
- **Order of Interbeing** (Plum Village) — 14 mindfulness trainings as principles; sub-organisations (ecological, social) federated under shared principles but otherwise uncoupled.
- **Sōka Gakkai** — lay Buddhist global network; reference with reservations (sectarian tendencies but real longevity).

## Belief systems as governance

- Religions and ideologies as governance patterns (what gets enforced, what gets rewarded, what the shared story is).
- Old Believers as case study of belief → economic structure → social outcome.
- The principle-governance parallel: AA ≈ lay sangha ≈ NGO constitution ≈ civic order.
- Artem's thesis: loss of principled governance at state level is one of the strongest metacrisis indicators worth arguing for.

## Neurophenomenology / embodied mind

- **Francisco Varela + Evan Thompson + Eleanor Rosch** — *The Embodied Mind* (1991).
- **Evan Thompson** — continuing the Varela programme; *Mind in Life*, *Waking, Dreaming, Being*; philosophical Buddhism meeting cognitive science.
- **William Irwin Thompson** — Evan's father; Lindisfarne Association (Canada, 1970s–80s); cross-tradition intellectual crucible (Tibetan Buddhists, Fritz Perls, etc. all in one room).

## Hemispheres — why the frame resists measurement

- **Iain McGilchrist** — *The Master and His Emissary*, *The Matter with Things*. Working hypothesis: "metacrisis" is inherently right-hemispheric in shape (pattern, relation, meaning) and therefore resists left-hemispheric demand for discrete measurable parameters — a useful lens on the Kapterev critique.

## Japanese Buddhist utopian communities

Early-20th-century quasi-Buddhist communities — not monastic, not sect-bound, but principled and practice-based. These are a structural reference for the "Sōka Gakkai in a new key" form.

- ***A Buddha Land in This World: Philosophy, Utopia, and Radical Buddhism*** — open-access monograph on JSTOR ([j.ctv2gz3zt3](https://www.jstor.org/content/oa_book_monograph/j.ctv2gz3zt3)). Shared by Viktor 2026-04-20 as the reference he had partly read.

## Offline community forms

- **Newspeak House** — London civic-tech co-living; small residential cohorts plus a busy event programme; hub for rationalists, EAs, LessWrong, Scott Alexander readers.
- **Lindisfarne Association** — William Irwin Thompson's studio.
- **Plum Village** — Thich Nhat Hanh's community in France.
- **Rufus Pollock / Second Renaissance / Life Itself** — Pollock's coliving in France; his personal site (separate from these projects) covers his Commons / Big Data / AI work more directly.

## Facilitation patterns

- **Climate Fresk** — open-source climate workshop; anyone can download and print the materials; free sessions run by certified facilitators worldwide; 2–3 hours of collaborative card-laying around a table. Reference for the *kit* form.
- **Pandemic** (board game, 2008) — cooperative-only; "no one can win alone, only everyone can lose" as pedagogy; historically the top-ranked game on BoardGameGeek.
- **Mindfulness card decks** — Vishvapani's 2008 deck referenced as a rare example of stackable cards *with rules*, not passive prompts. Most later decks lost the game layer.
