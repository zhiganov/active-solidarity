# concept

Living documents describing the emerging form. These are **provisional** — expect rewrites. Move stabilised thinking into `research/`.

## Working form — "Sōka Gakkai in a new key"

Viktor's phrase, adopted as working-title shape. A quasi-order — not a religion, not a company, not a school — built around:

- **Shared principles** (not values — see below)
- **Shared practices** — concrete, downloadable, runnable offline by anyone without gatekeepers
- **A commons** — open materials; Patreon / donation / grant-funded rather than course-by-course
- **Local autonomous cells** — each one holds the principles and develops its own adjacent work (ecological, contemplative, organisational, civic), loosely federated via the shared ground

Reference forms: Order of Interbeing (Plum Village), Alcoholics Anonymous, Russian Old Believers. All principle-based, all durable, none centrally controlled.

## Principles vs values

This distinction matters and should show up in everything.

- **Values** ≈ "it is nice when X, I prefer X." Fragile — put them under pressure (prison, war, scarcity) and they dissolve into emotion and self-preservation.
- **Principles** ≈ "under pressure we still do X because without X this breaks." Load-bearing.

Contemporary exemplar: AA's 12 Steps. Historical: Old Believer work ethic + mutual aid. Both survive pressure because they are principled, not sentimental.

## Tone

"Guerrilla activism" (Viktor) — open kit, partisan distribution. Seed envelopes buried in gardens. Meditation cards in mailboxes. Nothing that requires permission or a gatekeeper. The form is a lay-accessible analogue of what Nichiren did with chanting the title of the *Lotus Sutra*: a simple concrete practice plus a principled commitment, both portable, both repeatable.
